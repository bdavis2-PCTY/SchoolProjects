THE LIQUID STATE




The liquid phase normally comes after the solid phase, when enough heat is applied to change the objects phase. Liquids do not have a fixed shape, they will take the shape of the container that it is in, and if it's not in a container it will just spread out into a fairly flat surface. The molecules of a liquid are a lot looser than they are in a solid, which gives liquids the ability to change shapes.

Something that varies with liquids is how strong the molecules are connected. molecules will never be as strong as a liquid, however they can vary within liquids. For example, honey and water are both liquids, however, honey is a lot thicker than water.

When a liquid is cooled, it normally freezes into a solid, but when it heats up, it will evaporate into a gas.
