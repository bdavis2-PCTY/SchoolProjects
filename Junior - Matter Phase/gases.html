THE GAS STATE




Gas is the phase after liquid. Gases are everywhere, all around the universe, you even breathe a gas - Oxygen! There's all sorts of gases such as Carbon Dioxide, Helium, Nitrogen, and countless more! In a gas, the molecules do whatever they want. They float around and bounce into each other. A gas will take the shape of its container, even though you propbably won't be able to see it. However, since the molecules are moving a lot faster than they are in a solid or liquid, it does require quite a bit more space than it would for one of the other states of matter.

Gases hold a lot of energy which is why they are able to bounce all over the place and never stay in the same place.

When a gas is cooled, it will normally condensate into a liquid, but, if enough heat energy is added to the gas, it will ionize into plasma.
