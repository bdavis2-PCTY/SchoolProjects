Concept Goals

The implementation of Lean Six Sigma is sometimes considered one of the biggest changes to the service industry. Simply put, Lean Six Sigma aims to increase the profits of a company or business by eliminating variability, defects, and wastes that can undermine customer loyalty. Splitting up this statement reveals the four main goals of Lean Six Sigma: customer satisfaction, adding value, eliminate waste and increase flow, and capitalizing on opportunities. No matter the type of business or company, customer satisfaction is always a priority. Customers who are satisfied with a product or service are more likely to return to that same product or service instead of a different one, which will grant more profit in the future. Adding value to your product is another aspect of business that is required for all types of businesses. Adding value to your product can increase the trustworthiness of your product or business compared to competitors. When the value of a product increases, so does the trustworthiness. This can make customers more likely to buy your product over a competitor's. Eliminating waste and increasing flow are also important for every business. Businesses are more likely to be successful if they try to eliminate some of the eight forms of waste: defects, overproduction, waiting, non-utilized talent, transportation, inventory, motion, and extra processing. Eliminating unnecessary expenditures in these categories can increase the flow of a business, thus increasing effectiveness and profit. The last main goal, capitalizing on opportunities, focuses on taking advantage of value-generating opportunities and making profit from them. There are often activities or opportunities where a business can make a profit, but these are sometimes wasted. A business can improve these value-generating opportunities and actually make a profit from them if it is done correctly.
