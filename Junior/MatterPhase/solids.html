THE SOLID STATE




A solid is typically the "first" state of matter. When an object is solid, it has a defined shape and doesn't move or come apart easily. There are exceptions such as anamorphic solids (such as jello) that easily change shape but is still classified as a solid. Solids have different properties such as the strength of them, and how hard they are. Different solids can be used for different jobs.

In a solid, all the molecules are locked together, giving an object a fixed shape that may or may not be difficult to break. When something is frozen, it typically becomes a solid as long as the pressure and the temperature meets the requirements for the element. Solids don't have to be made of a specific element -- they can be a compound of multiple elements, such as concrete.

In most cases, on Earth, the next phase will be turning a solid melt into a liquid when enough heat is applied.
